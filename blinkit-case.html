Blinkit — Faster checkout for express shoppers

Optimized add-to-cart and express checkout to reduce abandonment and make millisecond-sensitive journeys feel faster.

Role: Product / UX Designer
Timeline: 1.5 months

Process

  1. Performed funnel deep-dive to isolate drop-off moments and identify high-friction touchpoints in add-to-cart and checkout.
  2. Built low-fi prototypes to test perceived speed patterns (optimistic UI, skeletons) and validated with moderated unmoderated sessions.Ran A/B experiments on microcopy and button prominence to measure lift in cold-start conversion.
  3. Collaborated with performance engineers to ensure perceived improvements didn't increase backend load.

Design highlights

Introduced a reduced-checkout flow with inline adders and clearer progress states. Visual skeletons and instant feedback created perceived speed while real-time validation reduced form errors.

Accessibility & internationalization

Ensured form inputs met WCAG contrast and label guidelines; localized strings to support multiple regions and tested RTL layouts for language parity.

Artifacts to show

  • Funnel heatmaps and clickstream segments
  • Prototype videos demonstrating perceived speed changes
  • Experiment result dashboards and decision logs

Outcome & learnings

Anonymized: measurable checkout conversion uplift in quick-purchase cohorts; lower abandonment on low-intent visits. Learned that perceived speed often drives behavior more than raw latency reductions.
