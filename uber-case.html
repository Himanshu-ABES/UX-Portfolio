Uber — Pickup confidence in dense cities

Reimagined map interactions and pickup confirmations to reduce rider confusion and cancellations in dense urban areas.

Role: Interaction Designer
Timeline: 2 months

Problem

Users experienced mismatched pickup points and ETA variance which led to failed pickups, cancellations and an increase in support tickets during peak hours.

Process

  • Conducted mixed-method research: ride-alongs, driver interviews, and session recordings to identify friction points.
  • Sketches → interactive prototypes (Figma) focusing on pickup pin clarity, contextual hints and microcopy.
  • Worked with Maps/Backend to test different pickup-state affordances and priority heuristics using feature flags.
  • Ran two pilots, collected pickup-change logs and qualitative feedback, and iterated rapidly on microinteractions.

Design highlights

Focused on clear affordances for pickup selection: a compact pin with contrast, a confirmation microflow, and a progressive disclosure for edge-case instructions (e.g., building entrances). Motion was used sparingly to show state changes without distracting drivers.

Accessibility & edge cases

Ensured color contrast for map pins, keyboard-accessible confirmation modals, and voice-over friendly labels so drivers with different abilities could rely on the same cues. Designed fallback flows for low-connectivity scenarios.

Artifacts to show

  • Annotated map flows and edge-case decision tree
  • Figma prototypes showing state transitions and microinteractions
  • Pilot dashboards with pickup-change and ticketing metrics

Key takeaways

Small, context-aware nudges delivered measurable improvements. Aligning product, maps engineering, and ops early reduced surprises during pilots and accelerated rollout.

Outcome

Anonymized: pickup-change rate fell ~10% in pilot areas; reduced support tickets related to pickup confusion. Riders reported higher confidence in pickup accuracy.
