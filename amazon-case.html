Amazon — Search & filtering for power users

Simplified complex search and filtering to reduce time-to-purchase for advanced shoppers through progressive filters and clearer taxonomy.

Role: Senior UX / Product Designer
Timeline: 3 months

Process

  • Conducted task-based usability testing with power users and novices to identify mismatches in filter taxonomy.
  • Iterated on progressive filter designs that supported both quick refinements and deep parameterization.
  • Validated taxonomy changes using clickstream segmentation and search-to-purchase funnels.

Design highlights

Built progressive filters that surface common refinements first and expose advanced options on demand. Focused on reducing cognitive load with clear labels, grouping and inline examples.

Artifacts to show

  • Taxonomy mapping and card-sorting results
  • Prototype demos of filter flows
  • Clickstream before/after and funnel visualizations

Accessibility & internationalization

Ensured filter controls are keyboard accessible, ARIA-labeled and reliable across localized content sets. Verified number and date formats for multiple locales.

Outcome

Anonymized: improved search-to-buy rate and fewer search attempts for advanced shoppers; clearer taxonomy reduced support queries about filtering behavior.
