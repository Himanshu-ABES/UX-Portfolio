YouTube — Intent-driven discovery

Improved video discovery by surfacing intent-driven recommendations and clearer affordances to increase engaged watch time.

Role: UX Designer
Timeline: 2 months

Approach

  • Performed cohort segmentation and task-based usability tests focused on discovery tasks.
  • Iterated on card hierarchy and information scent to improve relevance signals.
  • Used multivariate tests to evaluate layout variations and recommendation weights.

Design highlights

Refined card affordances to expose intent (watch, save, playlist). Introduced subtle micro-animations to indicate recommendation freshness and relevance cues without overwhelming the browsing experience.

Accessibility

Improved screen reader labels for cards, ensured keyboard focus order across dynamic lists, and tested color contrast across UI states. Also validated captions and discoverability for users relying on transcripts.

Artifacts to include

  • Cohort analysis reports and heatmaps
  • Prototype walkthroughs showing ranking and relevance changes
  • Accessibility test logs and remediation notes

Outcome

Anonymized: higher engaged-watch time and better task completion in tested cohorts; accessibility fixes reduced drop-off for assistive-tech users.
